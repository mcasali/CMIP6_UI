Choosing Climate Model Runs

Climate modelers employ a technique called ensembling to capture the range of possible climate states. An ensemble is a collection of model experiments of the same climate model, using the exact same physics, which differ slightly in their initial conditions. The different initial conditions results in different simulations for each ensemble member due to the nonlinearity of the climate system. Individual model experiments are called ensemble members, and the average is the ensemble average. Comparing the variation among ensemble members gives an estimate of the uncertainty associated with internal climate variability (IPCC 2007 WG-I Annex, p.945), while averaging the members gives a measure of the average model response to the imposed forcings.

Choose an ensemble member (Run 10, for example) if you want specific experiment data or you would like to compare multiple runs in order to measure variability in the model.

Choose the Ensemble Average if you want the mean state of the climate among all model experiments.
